Healthy Relationships

Meeting an Online Friend or Creator IRL

Internet communities are an incredible thing, but they have their unique issues. The line between reality and curated internet lives can be blurry. Here’s a few things to keep in mind when meeting with online friends and creators in person.

People often only share an idealized version of themselves online. Think about what you post online. What do you share? What do you avoid posting? Creators make these same considerations, often much more precisely. You only see part of the picture.

Social interactions can be very different online than in person. While someone may be very comfortable sharing personal information on a blog, they may not be as comfortable doing so in person.

People also have different definitions of what’s personal. Don’t force anyone - creator or otherwise - to share information they may not be comfortable with.

Always recognize and respect boundaries. Who do you allow to touch you in your daily life? Are there some friends you hug and others you don’t? Know and communicate these boundaries for yourself and respect the boundaries of others.

Some actions are never appropriate. If someone touches you without your consent, pressures you to be alone with them, says you owe them something, or does anything that makes you uncomfortable that is never okay. You have the right to say no, walk away, and to report it to a friend or authority figure.

Recognizing Abuse: Online and Off A Guide for Parents

Communication and Trust is Key

Talk early and often about relationships and healthy boundaries. Whether romantic or platonic, teens are still figuring out what appropriate boundaries are for them.

Discuss romantic and platonic relationships that you see on your favorite TV shows and ask your teen to note what they think are good and bad about them.

Avoid judgement. If a teen feels ashamed or thinks they may be viewed poorly, it can discourage them from reaching out.

Warning Signs

Early warning signs of bullying and abuse are commonly shrugged off. While these are some signs that something unhealthy may be happening, they do not necessarily prove abuse.

Unusual behavior with friends or family, such as cancelling regular plans in favor spending all of free time with one person.

Constant communication with a partner or friend to the point of distress or concern that other person may be upset if they aren’t available to talk, text, or hang out.

Excessive flattery or attention from a partner or friend, particularly if the relationship is new.

Changes in mood that are out of character, such as anxiety about getting to see their partner/friend.

Creating Reasonable Boundaries

When meeting online friends for the first time, the meeting should be in an open or public place. Explain the importance of choosing locations that aren’t isolated and that is easily accessible.

When at a convention, it’s a good practice to keep to public or common areas.

Explain the benefits of setting boundaries and allow teens to participate in setting them. This makes it much more likely that they’ll follow through.

Listen when they bring up concerns and work to find a compromise.
